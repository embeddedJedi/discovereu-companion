# Research: Erasmus+ Youth Grant Opportunities for DiscoverEU Companion

**Date:** 2026-04-13
**Requested by:** Emirhan Ekşi (solo founder, Türkiye-based, pre-launch open-source web app)
**Question:** Which Erasmus+ Youth actions (KA3/KA154, KA2/KA220, ESC) are realistically accessible to a Türkiye-based solo founder, and on what calendar?

## Answer (TL;DR)
All three pathways are open to Türkiye as an **EU Programme Country** associated to Erasmus+ and ESC. The fastest realistic money is **KA154 Youth Participation Activities** (lump sums, single-organisation possible, Feb/Oct deadlines). **KA220-YOU Cooperation Partnerships** (€120k / €250k / €400k) need a 3-country consortium and only a March deadline per year. **ESC Quality Label** is a prerequisite (not a grant), obtainable rolling with ~8 weeks evaluation.

> **Naming note:** what the user called "KA3 (€60k)" in the pivot memory is what the 2021–2027 programme guide actually names **KA154 — Youth Participation Activities**. The action sits inside KA1 (Learning Mobility), not KA3. The €60k figure the user remembered is a reasonable rule-of-thumb ceiling, not an official headline cap.

---

## 1. KA154 — Youth Participation Activities

- **Deadlines (Call 2026):** Round 1 — **12 February 2026, 12:00 Brussels**; Round 2 — **1 October 2026, 12:00 Brussels**. Analogous Feb + Oct pattern is expected for Call 2027 (confirmed publicly only after the 2027 Programme Guide drops, usually late November 2026).
- **Who can apply:** *Any public or private body* active in youth; **informal groups of young people** (min. 4 aged 13-30, one designated representative aged 18+) can also apply directly — no legal entity required. This is the single-founder-friendly path.
- **Türkiye eligibility:** Full — Türkiye is an Erasmus+ Programme Country associated to the Programme.
- **Grant model:** **Lump sum** based on a menu of activity unit costs. Typical project ceilings are in the **€10,000 – €60,000** range for a standalone project, set by activity composition (activity costs, facilitator/coach costs, inclusion support, preparatory visits, digital activity costs).
- **What the Commission expects as "deliverables":** youth-led activities that produce *concrete dialogue with decision-makers* — events, consultations, campaigns, digital participation tools, recommendations handed to public authorities. A companion web app qualifies if framed as **a tool to enable youth participation / dialogue around DiscoverEU policy**, paired with offline workshops and measurable engagement with EACEA or national youth councils.

## 2. KA220-YOU — Cooperation Partnerships (Youth)

- **Deadline 2026:** **5 March 2026, 12:00 Brussels** (confirmed). Expected **2027 deadline:** early March 2027 — no October round for KA220.
- **Consortium minimum:** **≥ 3 organisations from 3 different Programme Countries**. Türkiye counts as a Programme Country, so a TR-led consortium with 2 EU partners is valid. The coordinator must be legally established.
- **Lump sums:** **€120,000 / €250,000 / €400,000** — applicant picks one based on work plan. Confirmed in the 2026 KA2 lump-sum handbook published by Türkiye UA.
- **Digital platforms eligible?** Yes. Eligible activity types explicitly cover **digital tools, open educational resources, and online platforms** for youth. DiscoverEU Companion sits cleanly here.
- **Priorities that align:** Inclusion & Diversity; Digital Transformation; Environment & fight against climate change; Common values / civic engagement / participation. The project hits all four — materially improves scoring.

## 3. ESC Volunteering — Quality Label (not a grant, prerequisite)

- **Is it a grant?** No. It is **accreditation** that unlocks later volunteer-hosting grants (ESC51 Volunteering Projects).
- **Turkish nonprofit as Host?** Yes — Türkiye is a fully-participating ESC country. Turkish NGOs regularly hold Host and Lead Quality Labels.
- **Who to apply to:** Türkiye Ulusal Ajansı (for Programme Countries).
- **Timeline:** Applications **rolling / continuous**. National Agency evaluation typically **~8 weeks**. Label valid until 31 Dec 2027, expected renewable into 2028-2034 cycle.

## 4. Türkiye Ulusal Ajansı — national priorities 2026-2027

UA mirrors EU-level horizontal priorities (**Inclusion, Digital, Green, Participation in democratic life**) and in Türkiye adds emphasis on:
- Dijital dönüşümde kapsayıcılık ve eşitlik (digital inclusion / equity)
- Yeşil hareketlilik ve sürdürülebilirlik (green mobility)
- Gençlerin sivil ve demokratik katılımı
- Dezavantajlı gruplara (fewer-opportunities youth) erişim

DiscoverEU Companion checks every one of these — particularly since it lowers the DiscoverEU participation threshold for Turkish 18-year-olds, who compete under the Inclusion Action quota.

## Caveats & staleness

- KA154 per-project grant cap is computed from unit costs, not a single headline number. €60k is a rule-of-thumb ceiling for a well-scoped standalone project.
- KA2 second-round dates are agency-specific — Türkiye UA has historically NOT run a second KA220 round; confirm with `ua.gov.tr`.
- ESC funding rates were simplified mid-cycle; verify against the current 2026 ESC Programme Guide before committing.

## Recommended action sequence (solo-founder + small consortium)

1. **Before 1 Oct 2026 — KA154 Round 2 as an informal youth group or via a friendly Turkish youth NGO** (no legal entity needed). Scope: a **€30-50k lump-sum** project that funds dialogue events, translation sprints, and accessibility audits of the Companion — positioning it as a *youth-participation tool for DiscoverEU*. This is the realistic first win.
2. **Apply for an ESC Host Quality Label at Türkiye UA now** (rolling, ~8 weeks). Do this in parallel with the KA154 submission — it costs nothing and unlocks ESC51 funding to host 1-2 volunteers for platform localisation/community work in 2027.
3. **Build a 3-country KA220-YOU consortium for the 5 Mar 2027 deadline.** Recruit one EU youth NGO (DE/FR/NL preferred — strong EACEA signals) + one from a cohesion country (PT/RO/BG — scoring bonus). Target the **€250,000 lump sum**. Frame: "Open-source digital platform for inclusive DiscoverEU participation."
4. **Use the 2026-04-22 launch to seed evidence for the grant applications** — usage metrics, Turkish applicant testimonials, accessibility certifications, EACEA outreach letters. Every grant form has a "track record" section.
5. **Do not chase KA3 centralised actions** (EACEA-managed European Youth Together, etc.) as a solo applicant in the first cycle — those require 5+ partners in 5 countries and a proven coordinator. Re-evaluate for the 2028 call once the KA220 project is running.
